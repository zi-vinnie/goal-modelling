In this report we will be looking at goal data from five seasons of one
division (The Championship) of the English Football League. 
We will find a suitable statistical model and use it to make predictions for the 2024 season.
Then we will investigate how much difference home advantage makes.
\section{Finding a suitable model}

First we need to choose a suitable statistical model for the number of goals scored.
We know that the it should be discrete and non-negative, and
that there is no upper limit. It is fair to assume
that the event of a scoring a goal is independent of another goal being scored.
This makes the the Poisson distribution a possible suitable model for the number of goals scored.

Let $X$, $Y$ be the random variable representing the number of home and away goals respectively.
Then $X \sim \text{Poisson}(\lambda_h)$ and $Y \sim \text{Poisson}(\lambda_a)$ follow a poisson distribution with rate parameters $\lambda_h$ and $\lambda_a$ respectively.
The first moment of a poisson distribution is $\lambda$.

\begin{equation}
E[X] = \lambda_h \quad \text{and} \quad E[Y] = \lambda_a
\end{equation}

This gives us $\hat{\lambda}_h \approx 1.41$ and $\hat{\lambda}_a \approx 1.13$. We can then plot a histogram of the home goals
and away goals and overlay their respective poisson distributions to see if they fit.
\begin{figure}[h]
  \centering
  \begin{minipage}{0.48\textwidth}
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{../Plots/Question1/HomeGoals.pdf}
    \caption{Proportion histogram of home goals with Poisson distribution overlay}
    \label{fig:home_goals}
  \end{minipage}\hfill
  \begin{minipage}{0.48\textwidth}
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{../Plots/Question1/AwayGoals.pdf}
    \caption{Proportion histogram of away goals with Poisson distribution overlay}
    \label{fig:away_goals}
  \end{minipage}
\end{figure}

As we can see from the plots, the Poisson distributions fit the data well.
From this we can conclude that the Poisson distribution is a suitable model for the number of goals scored.
